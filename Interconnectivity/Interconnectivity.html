The Internet As A Framework For Global Interactions

How the Internet provided a framework for global interactions, Nov 4, 2025
Fake Image

According to its creator Tim Berners-Lee, the World Wide Web - or in other words, the method which the majority of people use to access Internet communication services to this day - was originally created as a "universal linked information system"1. Developed at CERN, it was initially intended mainly as a means for researchers to share information across the world, much like the Internet itself, which was developed for the same purpose 2 3.

Over time, however, due to the necessity of allowing public contributors to create their own browsers to allow for faster development and the wish to keep the service publically accessible, the service was released to the public, this leading to the communication between users via various different means across the Internet and particularly the World Wide Web that we see today: recall that web pages such as forums, for example, are still hosted via the World Wide Web, as demonstrated by the "www" at the beginning of their web addresses, and that it is the Voice Over Internet Protocol (VOIP) which allows us to call others across the world. 2

The Internet As A Conduit For Cultural and Subcultural Exchanges

Title description, Sep 2, 2020
Fake Image

With time, the Internet would expand across the world, connecting people of various different cultures speaking various different languages, who inevitably interacted via a variety of means, creating websites such as the WordReference Forums which allow for the exchange of cultural and linguistic knowledge, among other, less serious forums such as Reddit.

The Internet has also, almost since its conception, facilitated subcultural exchanges via forums such as Reddit (among other locations) which have connected together various communities and allowed them to grow, as discussed in the context of the LGBTQ+ community in Japan by Erica Friedman 4 in her book "By Your Side".
