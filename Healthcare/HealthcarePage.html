Improving physical capabilities



In the last few decades, the physical limits of technology have been pushed far beyond its limits many times.

Some iconic examples:

These inventions have all had a profound impact on medical technology.

  • Electron Microscope (1931): allowed visualisation of viruses and sub-cellular structures.

  • Dialysis Machine (1943): enabled life support for patients with kidney failure.

  • Cardiac Pacemaker (1954): regulates heart rhythms.

  • Medical Ultrasound (1958): imaging using high-frequence sound waves.

  • MRI scanner (1978): even more detailed soft tissue imaging without the use of harmful radiation.

  • 3D Printing (1985): allowed medical institutions to print custom models for surgery.

Advancements in Software

Some examples...

Medical diagnosis and imaging are two crucial areas in which computing has made great progress. Advanced algorithms can analyse medical pictures like MRIs, CT scans, and X-rays with incredible accuracy, assisting doctors in detecting diseases and abnormalities early on.

Machine learning techniques can also anticipate patient outcomes using data from similar cases, allowing healthcare providers to make better informed judgements about treatment approaches.

Processing Power

Another aspect where computing contributes to healthcare is in the management and analysis of vast amounts of patient data. Electronic Health Records (EHRs) streamline the storage and retrieval of patient information, enabling healthcare professionals to access comprehensive medical histories promptly.

Data analytics techniques can uncover valuable insights from EHRs, such as identifying trends in disease prevalence, optimizing resource allocation, and even predicting disease outbreaks.
